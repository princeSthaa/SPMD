## 1. Introduction
### 1.1 Background
The integration of Information Technology in the healthcare sector has become a necessity to ensure patient safety and operational efficiency. Manual prescription handling often leads to errors in dispensing, drug-allergy interactions, and inventory mismanagement. The **Smart Prescription and Medication Dispensing System (SPMD)** is designed to automate the clinical workflow, from doctor consultation to pharmacy fulfillment, ensuring "Clinical Precision."

## 2. Statement of the Problem
Traditional medical systems suffer from several bottlenecks:
*   **Safety Risks:** Difficulty in tracking patient allergy profiles in real-time during prescribing.
*   **Inventory Inefficiency:** Manual tracking of medicine batches often results in stock expiration or shortages.
*   **Role Confusion:** Overlapping responsibilities between doctors and pharmacists can lead to clinical accountability issues.
*   **Lack of Auditability:** Difficulty in tracking the forensic trail of clinical decisions.

---

## 3. Objectives
### 3.1 General Objective
To develop and implement a robust, role-based clinical management system that ensures safe medication dispensing and intelligent inventory tracking.

### 3.2 Specific Objectives
*   To implement an **Allergy Safety Engine** that provides real-time warnings and hard blocks during prescribing.
*   To develop a **FEFO (First-Expired-First-Out)** dispensing logic to optimize inventory turnover.
*   To establish a clear **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)** system for Doctors, Pharmacists, and Patients.
*   To create a **Forensic Audit Trail** for tracking all clinical actions and dispensing records.
*   To gain hands-on experience in **ASP.NET Core 10.0**, **Entity Framework Core**, and **Clean Architecture**.

---

## 4. Methodology
The project follows the **Agile Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)**, focusing on iterative development and clinical validation.

### 4.1 Requirement Analysis
Identification of key stakeholders (Doctors, Pharmacists, Patients) and their clinical requirements.
### 4.2 System Design
*   **Database Design:** SQL Server with EF Core (Repositories and Services).
*   **UI/UX Design:** Razor Pages with a Navy Primary "Clinical Precision" aesthetic.
### 4.3 Implementation
Using C# and .NET 10.0 to build the core clinical engine and inventory management.
### 4.4 Testing and Validation
Unit testing of safety checks and FEFO logic, followed by user acceptance testing (UAT).
